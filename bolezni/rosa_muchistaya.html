Powdery mildew

Press to see the photograph

Powdery mildew usually appears in mid-July in the form of a white bloom on the surface of the leaves (they seem to be dusted with flour); appears later in wet years. It spreads rapidly by air movement. Dry and hot weather is a favourable condition for the development of the disease; therefore Central Asia is considered the birthplace of powdery mildew. The withering of beets on hot days reduces the resistance of plants to powdery mildew. Frequent rainfall and sprinkler irrigation reduce disease incidence. Affected leaves gradually turn yellow and die off, this leads to a decrease in the yield of root crops by 10–20% and their sugar content up to 1.5%.

Protection methods

Agrotechnical method

Use of hybrids resistant to this disease, observance of crop rotation, spatial isolation between fields with beet crops, improvement of water regime
