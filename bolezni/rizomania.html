Rhizomania

Press to see the photograph

Of the viral diseases, rhizomania (fibroses) is considered significant. Caused by the beet necrotic yellow vein virus (BNYVV). A characteristic symptom of this disease is the increased spread of lateral roots from the main root. The root crop remains small and often compressed shallowly below the soil level. Leaves curl, wither much faster, and remain small, narrow, light green with long petioles. A symptom of "beardedness" appears on the roots. When the root crop is cut, blackened vascular bundles are found. The losses caused by rhizomania are very serious: the harvest is halved. This virus lives in soil and is carried by the soil fungus Polymyxa beta. The infection does not disappear in the affected regions for 12–15 years. Symptoms of rhizomania can be confused with those that occur when a beet nematode is affected, and accurate results are only given by tests in the laboratory. Rhizomania also has host plants from the haze family (quinoa, white gauze, etc.), but the infection persists in the soil for a long time and without them. Therefore, compliance with crop rotation does not make it possible to get rid of the infection.

Protection methods

Agrotechnical method

Compliance with crop rotation, the use of sustainable hybrids.
