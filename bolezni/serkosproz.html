Cercospora

Cercosporosis - the source of infection can be not only beets, but also beetroot, quinoa, sorrel, alfalfa, etc. It appears on the leaves in the form Rounded light brown spots with a reddish rim 2–4 mm in size. With a strong lesion, the spots merge, and the leaf plate darkens and dies off. For the development of cercospora, the most favourable temperature is 15 ° C and above with a relative humidity of more than 70%, for example, when it rains for 3-4 consecutive days or there is abundant dew. Under such conditions, the incubation period for the development of the fungus is 6–7 days. If conditions are less favourable, it lasts 40 days. Favourable conditions in the early development of cercospora contributes to the complete withering away of the leaf apparatus. In addition, although in the future new leaves are formed, the reserve substances of the root crop are spent on this, which leads to a decrease in yield by 20-30% and sugar content by 2% or more. With a late manifestation of cercospora (1 month before harvesting), chemical treatment of crops is economically inexpedient. The economic threshold of the harmfulness of cercosporosis occurs when 5% of plants are affected before August 1, after which it becomes necessary to fight the disease. The registration of diseased plants is carried out as follows: they pass the field diagonally, tearing at equal distances from one hundred plants one leaf from the middle tier (middle tier, but also not old). If there is one spot on the sheet, it is considered affected. If out of a hundred leaves, five turns out to be affected by cercospora, then the threshold of harmfulness has been reached, and treatment with fungicides is necessary. Practice has shown that in dry years, plants first fall ill with powdery mildew, and by the end of the growing season (second half of September), when the air humidity increases and abundant dew falls, they become sick with cercospora.

Protection methods

Agrotechnical method

    Correct crop rotation, observance of spatial isolation from possible sources of infection, destruction of weeds along the edges of fields and deep ploughing of plant residues with a low with a skimmer after harvesting beets.
