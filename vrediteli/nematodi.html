Nematodes

Click to show the picture

Among the most dangerous pests of sugar beet are nematodes - beet, gall, stem, etc. Nematodes are microscopic thin round worms. Beet nematode size: length 0.41–0.44 mm, thickness 0.02–0.25 mm; garlic - length 0.4–1.3 mm, thickness 0.27–0.80 mm (in females). They parasitize in the tissues of the small lateral roots of beets, depleting plants, disrupting normal functions, which negatively affects the productivity of plants. It remains in the soil for a long time, and the amount of damage increases with the saturation of crop rotations with sugar beets and other host plants, which include cabbage plants such as winter and spring rape, all types of cabbage, radishes, radishes, etc. roots reaches 50% or more.

The characteristic signs of nematode damage are the chloroticity of the leaves, their external yellowing, wilting of plants even with sufficient moisture, then drying and dying off. The main root is poorly developed, has numerous lateral roots with lemon-shaped cysts, and gradually decays. The harmfulness threshold for beet nematodes is considered to be 500 eggs + larvae per 100 g of soil sample.
