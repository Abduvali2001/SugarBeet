The winter moth belongs to the gnawing moths and develops in 2 generations. Partially 3rd generation, depending on living conditions.

Caterpillars of the 1st generation harm in late May-early June. The caterpillar gnaws at the root neck and can destroy up to 10-15 plants (1 caterpillar) per night.

On a more developed plant, the caterpillar gnaws at the leaf petiole. During the day, the caterpillar keeps in the soil curled up into a ring, with the onset of dusk it activates. With a number of 0.2-0.5 individuals per 1 m of row length, processing must be carried out. It is recommended to spray in the evening and at night.

Protection method

Agrotechnical method

Months August-September carry out pre-ploughing irrigation and chilly ploughing. 1st watering reduces the number of pests (1st generation of pests). They also use entomophage - trichogramma.
