Spider mite

Click to show the picture

The most harmful of these on sugar beets is currently the spider mite. Appears in the second half of summer, moves to beets from weeds. It settles on the underside of the leaves, entangling with the finest cobwebs. Gives up to 14 generations. In autumn, females acquire a reddish-orange colour, in summer they are green, 0.3–0.5 mm in size. Each female can lay over 100 eggs. The mite sucks up plant juices. Sucking sites are silvery white, and the upper side of the leaf becomes yellow, which later spreads to the entire leaf, leading to its premature drying. With a strong spread, yield losses can reach 7–10 t / ha, and sugar content - by 2–2.5%.

Protection method

Agrotechnical

Compliance with crop rotation. It is also necessary to keep the fields free of weeds.
