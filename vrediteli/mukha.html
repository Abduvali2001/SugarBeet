Miner fly

Click to show the picture

The beet miner fly also damages beet leaves. They lay eggs on leaves in heaps, and the female can lay up to 100 eggs. The hatched larvae take root in the pulp of the leaf; depending on the size of the clutch, several larvae feed on the leaf. The upper skin of the leaf swells up, and subsequently dies off and breaks. With a large amount of damage, plants lag behind in growth and give less yield.
