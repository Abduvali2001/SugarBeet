The use of seeds treated with insecticides (insecticides are chemical means of protection against pests) eliminated the risk of damage to beets by the beet flea in the period from germination to 3-4 pairs of leaves. Although there were times when it was enough to skip 5–6 days during germination, and the flea, having populated the sowing en masse, damaged the seedlings and their growth point so much that they had to resell entire fields.
