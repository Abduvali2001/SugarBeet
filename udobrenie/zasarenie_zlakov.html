What to do if it is clogged with cereals?

Anti-cereal herbicides or graminicides (in Latin, cereal - Graminea) are widely represented: Zellek-super (d.v. Galaxifop-R-methyl), Miura (d.v. chizalofop-P-ethyl), Heler (d.v. quizalofop -P-tefuryl), Fuzilad forte (d.v. fluazafop-P-butyl) and others. The sensitivity of cereals to them is high. For effective action, it is necessary that annual cereal weeds have 3-4 leaves, and perennial weeds reach 10-15 cm in height. Accordingly, the dosages of chemicals for weed control are different: the older the weeds, especially perennial ones, the more developed they are, the higher the rate of application of the drug.

To successfully combat weeds, you must adhere to some rules:

  • The best biological effectiveness of herbicides is achieved at a surface air temperature of 12–25 ° C; and at temperatures above 25 ° C and strong solar radiation, it is impossible to spray, there is a risk of damage to the crop. In such cases, it is better to spray in the morning or evening hours, you can at night.
  • In order to avoid drift by the wind, it is necessary to apply herbicides at a wind speed of less than 5 m / s.
  • If abundant dew falls out, when it drips from the leaves, it is impossible to spray.
  • • If the applied preparations do not contain oil emulsions, the addition of oils at the rate of 0.5–2 l / ha can improve the penetrating effect of herbicides through the waxy layer of leaves in dry weather.
  • • it is advisable to finish the treatment 4 hours before the rain falls. If caught in the rain, then re-work no earlier than 4 days later.
  • • for the preparation and mixing of working solutions of pesticides, the water must be clean and of good quality. Dirty water binds active substances (glyphosate, diquat are sensitive). If a 1 som coin is poorly viewed at the bottom of an ordinary bucket (10-12 liters), the water is considered dirty. Hard water (high content of calcium and magnesium salts) can lead to precipitation of some chemical elements (glyphosate, 2,4 D amine salt, clopyralid). Soap dissolves poorly in hard water. At pH> 8 (high alkalinity of water), pesticides (glyphosate, clopyralid) degrade and reduce their effectiveness.
  • • water should not be cold or hot - this affects the solubility and action of chemical elements. If you have to use poor quality water, spraying should be done immediately after preparing the solution.

To monitor the effectiveness of herbicides on the field, it is necessary to leave control areas (1-2 points) measuring 0.5x 0.5 m or 1.0x 1.0 m, not treated with a chemical (they must be covered with foil when spraying). This allows you to identify dead weed species, as well as trace the emergence of new weeds.
