Concentration calibration method

The concentration method should only be used when the method of spraying on a certain area (ml/ha) is not practical. These situations include: spraying very small plants until they completely cover the field; or spraying individual plants or trees. In such a situation, you will spray only small plants, and not the entire field, and save preparations. This method can also be used when spraying small areas, such as seedlings in nurseries or greenhouses, when plant density is high and the area is small.

When using this method, there is always a risk of using too much of the drug. Where possible, spray with a calibrated sprayer, using the area spraying method for more accurate pesticide use (g or ml per hectare). This is a more accurate method than the concentration method. Always check the label for specific application rates for different pests and diseases..

For your information, 10 liters of working solution should be enough to spray a completely covered area of ​​100-200 m with plants.2.
